# California Homelessness: Facts Summary

---

## Spending vs. Results

**California has spent $37 billion on homelessness since 2019 while the crisis has worsened.**

1. Homelessness increased 24% since Governor Newsom's 2019 "Marshall Plan" ([Hoover Institution](https://www.hoover.org/research/california-homelessness-rises-187000-perhaps-many-more-despite-37-billion-spent))
2. Current homeless population: ~187,000 people ([Hoover Institution](https://www.hoover.org/research/california-homelessness-rises-187000-perhaps-many-more-despite-37-billion-spent))
3. California's homeless population grew 31% from 2010-2020 while the national rate decreased 9% ([Ludwig & Rauh, Hoover](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))

---

## Accountability Gap

**Three separate audits found California cannot track where homelessness money goes or whether programs work.**

1. State Auditor (2024): Cal ICH "has not consistently tracked and evaluated the State's efforts" ([CA State Auditor](https://www.auditor.ca.gov))
2. Federal audit (2024): Department of Housing received lowest possible score for fraud detection; $319.5M at risk ([HUD OIG](https://www.hudoig.gov))
3. Legislative Analyst's Office: Legislature lacks "basic information on program outputs" ([LAO](https://lao.ca.gov))

---

## Housing First Failure

**Permanent supportive housing units grew 150% while homelessness continued to rise.**

1. PSH units increased 150% from 2010-2020; homelessness still grew ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
2. After 5 years in Housing First programs, only 36% remain housed ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
3. In SF PSH study: 25% of participants died, 20%+ returned to homelessness ([SF Chronicle, cited in Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))

---

## Mental Health & Substance Abuse

**Approximately 75% of chronically homeless have mental illness, substance abuse, or both.**

1. 76% of LA County unsheltered homeless affected by mental illness, substance abuse, or physical illness ([LA Times/2019 PIT survey](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
2. Drug overdose is the leading cause of death among homeless in LA and SF ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
3. 83% of CA counties have zero chemical dependency beds; 43% have no psychiatric inpatient care ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))

## What Works

**Contingency management and drug courts show strong results in studies.**

1. 86% of contingency management studies report significant treatment effects ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
2. VA contingency management program achieved 92% negative drug tests ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
3. Drug court participants are 3x less likely to be re-arrested ([Ludwig & Rauh](https://www.hoover.org/research/homelessness-california-causes-and-solutions))
